Healthcare Service Gaps in Singapore

Healthcare Service Gap in Singapore is a data visualziation project that identifies neighborhoods in Singapore that with potential challenges in accessing healthcare service options.

Using government open data, the project maps clinic locations and analyzes accessibility by measuring a 400-meter walkshed from HDB flats. This helps highlight areas where healthcare options may be limited.

In addition, a crowdsourced map on the Ushahidi platform allows users to share their medical consultation experiences at clinics, providing valuable insights into service quality and accessibility.
